Kathy's Blueberry and Lemon Cake

Submitted by Lillian Dearing

This is one of my favorite desserts for a summer's day, brought to you from my mother's personal recipe notes. She made it in early August with handpicked blueberries that we had collected from a local orchard. The light and sugary frosting is my mother's own recipe, while the cake batter was adapted from Lindsay Funston's recipe, as linked below. The cake's flavor gets better with age, and is at its prime after a day of refrigeration, accompanied by an iced tea.

Cake Batter Ingredients

  • 1 18 oz. vanilla cake mix (including box ingredients)
  • 2 cups freshly handpicked highbush blueberries
  • 3 tbsp. flour
  • 1 tbsp. lemon syrup

Frosting Ingredients

  • 2 sticks of melted, locally made butter
  • 2 to 3 cups confectioners sugar
  • 1 tbsp. lemon syrup
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 1/4 cup locally harvested milk

Cake Batter Instructions

  1. Set oven to 350°
  2. Use a non-stick bundt cake pan
  3. Pour cake mix ingredients into a bowl and mix, adding lemon syrup
  4. Mix the blueberries and flour in a separate bowl and blend with cake mixture
  5. Pour the ingredients into the bundt cake pan and bake for 20 minutes
  6. Allow the cake to cool for 10 minutes
  7. Frost the cake

Frosting Instructions

  1. Mix the ingredients together, in order of the frosting instructions
  2. whisk the ingredients by hand for about 5 minutes, (or with an electric mixer for 2 minutes) until the frosting is dense enough to be applied to the cake
